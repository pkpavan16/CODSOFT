Content-Based Filtering (CB)

Content-based systems suggest items based on their properties and what you've liked in the past. If you rate sci-fi movies highly, the system understands you like sci-fi and recommends other sci-fi movies.

1. Vector Representation

Every item is represented as a vector of genres. For instance, in a system with genres [Action, Sci-Fi, Drama], a pure Action film is [1, 0, 0], and a Sci-Fi Action movie is [1, 1, 0].

2. Creating your Preference Vector

We compile your ratings ($R_i$). For each rated item, we take its genre vector and scale it by a rating weight (we use $Rating - 2.5$). If you rate a movie 5 stars, we add $+2.5$ times its vector. If you rate it 1 star, we subtract $-1.5$ times its vector. Summing and normalizing these gives your **Preference Vector** (representing your genre affinities).

3. Calculating Cosine Similarity

We compare your Preference Vector ($\vec{U}$) against each unrated item's genre vector ($\vec{I}$) using the Cosine Similarity formula:

$$\text{Similarity}(\vec{U}, \vec{I}) = \cos(\theta) = \frac{\vec{U} \cdot \vec{I}}{\|\vec{U}\| \|\vec{I}\|}$$

This outputs a score between -1.0 (opposite interests) and 1.0 (exact match). Items are sorted by this similarity score.

User-Based Collaborative Filtering (CF)

Collaborative filtering is based on the idea that if users agreed in the past, they will agree in the future. Instead of item attributes, it analyzes patterns in ratings from a community of users.

1. Adjusting for User Rating Bias

Different users rate differently: some are generous (average rating 4.5), others are harsh (average rating 2.5). To align ratings, we compute each user's average rating ($\bar{R}_u$) and **mean-center** their rating vectors ($R_{u, i} - \bar{R}_u$). Unrated items are set to 0 (neutral/mean).

2. Calculating User Similarity

We compute Centered Cosine Similarity (Pearson Correlation) between you and other users. A score of $1.0$ means your tastes are perfectly aligned, $0.0$ means no correlation, and $-1.0$ means opposite tastes.

3. Predicting Ratings (Weighted Average)

To predict your rating ($P_{u, p}$) for an unrated item $p$, we take your average rating ($\bar{R}_u$) and add a weighted average of other users' centered ratings for item $p$, weighted by their similarity to you ($w_{u, v}$):

$$P_{u, p} = \bar{R}_u + \frac{\sum_{v \in \text{Neighbors}} w_{u, v} \cdot (R_{v, p} - \bar{R}_v)}{\sum_{v \in \text{Neighbors}} |w_{u, v}|}$$

This projects a rating from 1 to 5. We recommend the items with the highest predicted ratings.
